What is collabolative outsourcing
Collaborative outsourcing refers to a business arrangement where organizations outsource certain functions or processes to external service providers while maintaining a collaborative partnership. Unlike traditional outsourcing, which typically involves a more transactional relationship, collaborative outsourcing emphasizes a closer collaboration and integration between the client organization and the service provider. There is a higher degree of cooperation, coordination, and communication between both parties.

The future of workplace design; an example of LinkedIn hybrid office

by A. Lungu    18/05/2023
Architectural Designer Freelancer

Project by NBBJ Architecture & Design firm, US

The future of workplace design is evolving, and one of the emerging trends is the hybrid office model, which combines remote work with in-person collaboration. LinkedIn is one company that has adopted this model, and its approach to workplace design provides insight into the future of the hybrid office.

...continue reading

With more than 70 different types of sitting, LinkedIn's hybrid office is designed to support a range of work styles and needs.  The entry to the building presents a more social arrangement with a cafe just right at the reception,  while as you go further into the building it becomes more focused. The design incorporates flexible workstations, co-working spaces, meeting rooms with conference rooms looking like living rooms, and communal areas, as well as technology that supports remote collaboration.

In order to improve employee well been, LinkedIn has included social spaces in its workplace design. These social spaces include amenities such as a cafe, fitness center, and a game room that encourages employees to take breaks and socialize. In addition, it incorporates features such as natural light, greenery, and ergonomic furniture supporting different types of work for a given time period.

Overall, LinkedIn's hybrid office demonstrates that workplace design is evolving to support the needs and preferences of employees in a rapidly changing work environment. The future of workplace design is likely to include more flexible and adaptable spaces, technology that supports remote collaboration, and a focus on employee well-being.
